The leadership of a charity is made up of several roles, including the chairperson, the board, and charity trustees:

Chairperson

The chairperson is often the main spokesperson for the charity. They may delegate some of their public duties to the manager, CEO, or public relations officer.

Board

The board is responsible for the charity's strategic leadership. They ensure the charity has clear goals and a strategy to achieve them. The board also agrees on the charity's values, vision, and reputation.

Charity trustees

Charity trustees are legally responsible for the charity and exercise control over it. In a company charity, they may also be known as directors or board members.

Charity leaders should have the following qualities:

(i) Empathy: Charity leaders should be empathetic and understand the needs of the communities they serve.

(ii) Resilience:Charity leaders should be resilient and able to endure challenges.

(iii)Adaptability: Charity leaders should be adaptable and able to find what works best for the organization.

(iv)Culture of trust: Charity leaders should be able to create a culture of trust.

(v)Investing in people: Charity leaders should invest in people.
